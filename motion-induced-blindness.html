Motion-Induced Blindness

Watch as clearly visible objects disappear from your awareness while you stare at them. Your brain actively suppresses what it deems unimportant.

What to Do

Fixate steadily on the central cross. Do not move your eyes. After a few seconds of staring, the yellow target dots will begin to disappear and reappear from your peripheral vision.

The dots are always there and never actually vanish. Your visual system is actively suppressing them.

The Philosophical Point

Motion-induced blindness reveals that consciousness actively filters and suppresses unchanging peripheral information. Your brain decides what reaches awareness based on what it considers relevant.

This demonstrates that perception is not passive reception but active construction. The stable, unmoving targets are deemed "uninteresting" compared to the dynamic motion field, so your brain literally removes them from conscious experience.

Tips

  • Keep your gaze fixed on the central cross - do not look at the yellow dots
  • Be patient - the effect takes 5-15 seconds to begin
  • The dots may disappear one at a time or all together
  • Try adjusting the rotation speed to find what works best for you
